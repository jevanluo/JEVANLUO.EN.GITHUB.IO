Quantitative Education Talks

Posted on 2018-01-18 |

The reason I started this blog is to record my journey as education researcher, with a special focus on quantitative research mehtods.

Education research does not have to be quantitative, or the research itself does not to be so-called scientific or even empirical. Research methods are idea tools to persuade and convince, and clearly there are various preferences to the idea tools in different times. Today the most favoring tool happens to be quantitative methods.

On the other hand, that we need quantitative methods to see more in education also results from the mass education. The educated population increased due to the short relative long peace in most part of area on earth. The subjects of research eventually have reached the point to take the advantage of modern statistis.

The third reason, of course, is the development of data-collecting technologies. Education researchers have benefitted a lof from the large-scale family surveys like sociologists and economics, or from the designed laboratory experiments like psychologists. However, technologies to collect real-world education environments data, such as classroom data and learning behavior data, such as student learning data both online and offline are not accessible until now. It seems research on those actual education data would become new fashion as long as the first two trend hold.

Following fashion is one thing, however, the fashion itself helps us know more about our learning and then improve it. It’s especially important today because since most of humans got educated, what we could further the good deeds of our intellectual empire?
